The Catch!!

There are things we need to be sure of before proceeding to port... I mean minimum requirements...

This is the bare minimum spec required. If you cannot meet these, please don't try, as it will likely lead to frustration.

  • A Linux Environment (Recommended: Ubuntu 20.04 LTS).
  • A decent Internet Connection (Better if it's a 1Mb/s plan at the least). Because sources can be very large.
  • CPU: 4 cores and 8 threads is the bare minimum, or else it takes an eternity to build.
  • RAM: 8GB Minimum (+ ZRAM + SWAP optional, but it is better to have 16GB of RAM).
  • Storage: An SSD is better, but an HDD will work. 250GB is a decent amount of space.
  • Some Linux knowledge and basic ideas about Git.
  • You need to have the device **TREES** (Device Tree, Kernel Tree, and Vendor Tree).
  • A GitHub Account (Preferably with your device trees forked/imported).

Alright! Now we're talking. You're good to go!

Setup & Pre-Requisites

Step 1: Just make sure you satisfy the minimum requirements and have your build environment ready!

Porting GSIs

Step 2: Learn to port a Generic System Image (GSI) as a starting point.

Recovery Porting

Step 3: Build and port a custom recovery like TWRP or OrangeFox for your device.

Debugging Recovery & Bootloops

Step 4: Understand how to read logs (logcat, dmesg) to fix issues when your device won't boot.

AVB/Permissive Fixes

Step 5: Learn to patch AVB (Android Verified Boot) and set the kernel to permissive for testing.

Finalizing and Sharing

Step 6: Clean up your work and prepare to share it with the community.

Blindly following guides without understanding the steps is not recommended. It lacks quality and you won't learn much. However, this is how we all start. Just don't make this a habit, and never release untested ports.

Golden Rule 1

Never PM developers for help unless you have truly exhausted all other options. They do quality work and are better left undisturbed!

Golden Rule 2

Never ever ever build as ROOT! (At this point, you should know what this means).

Golden Rule 3

Don't be afraid to ask for help! There are many communities like the Android Porting Telegram group. Check the rules and ask for help there.
